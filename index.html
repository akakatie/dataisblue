Data is blue

About

I am a researcher and creative practitioner who is interested in people, art, and data. Data visualisation is both the confluence of these interests and a tool that helps me make sense of the world.

I make a lot of bar charts in my day job, so I use my personal creative practice as a way of experimenting with the languages of data visualisation and exploring qualitative representations of data. I’m interested in how data is used to represent people and our experiences.

Some of my recent works on this theme:

  • #MyMatrescenceProject – using “data-less” data visualisation to explore my experiences becoming a mother.
  • A death, a dot, a datapoint – a data story that visualises 15 years of grief after my father’s death.

You can keep up with my work on either Bluesky or Instagram.

Why data is blue?

The presenter of first data storytelling workshop I ever attended made joke about stock images of data always being blue. A few years ago when I was learning python, I had a go at seeing if it was true. Around the same time, I found out .blue was a top level domain so I bought the domain because it made me laugh and I hoped I’d do something with it one day. I still haven’t really found what I want to do with this domain but it houses all my project subdirectories so it needs some content.
